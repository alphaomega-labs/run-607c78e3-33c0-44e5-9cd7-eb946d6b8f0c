This distillation synthesizes the current corpus around a concrete objective: optimize exact neurosymbolic ASP inference by combining semantics-preserving rewrites with backend routing. The source base spans foundational treewidth and knowledge compilation theory (src_001, src_002, src_003), weighted and algebraic counting formalisms (src_004, src_007, src_009, src_039), neural-probabilistic logic frameworks (src_011, src_012, src_016, src_019, src_033), structural hardness analyses for ASP/treewidth (src_045, src_046, src_047, src_048), and recent surveys that map robustness, uncertainty, and generalization concerns (src_028, src_029, src_035, src_041, src_044). The central challenge for AutoTW-ASP is not only to prove that exact inference can be accelerated in theory, but to define an optimization policy that is algorithmically explicit, robust under structural variance, and faithful to stable-model semantics.

A first consensus thread concerns structure as the dominant driver of exact-inference complexity. Bodlaender's classic result (src_001) establishes that low fixed treewidth enables linear-time decomposition procedures, while the knowledge compilation map (src_002) formalizes why language choice mediates the practical tractability of downstream queries. DPLL trace compilation (src_003) then operationalizes this insight: search traces can be recorded as DAG artifacts with membership in tractable compiled languages under constraints. For ASP-facing counting and compilation, this structure-first view is sharpened by src_045, src_046, src_047, src_048, and src_039, each showing that cycle structure, SCC growth, and decomposition quality directly modulate runtime exponents or preprocessing utility. Across these papers, equations vary, but the shared message is stable: tractability changes when structure changes.

A second consensus thread is that exact probabilistic reasoning can be standardized as weighted counting over compiled or searched Boolean forms, but only when semantics-preserving encodings are available. Source src_004 positions weighted model counting (WMC) as the unifying exact-inference reduction for probabilistic models encoded into weighted CNF. Sources src_007 and src_009 extend this operationally into probabilistic logic programs, connecting MAP/marginal tasks to weighted Boolean formulas. In neurosymbolic settings, src_011 and src_012 adapt this lineage differently: DeepProbLog (src_011) emphasizes neural predicates over ProbLog semantics, whereas NeurASP (src_012) grounds probabilities over stable models of transformed programs. Both approaches maintain end-to-end training paths, but they inherit the same bottleneck identified earlier: symbolic exactness becomes expensive when grounding and structural width increase.

The main contradiction in the corpus is not whether exactness matters, but where to place the exactness-efficiency tradeoff. Sources src_012, src_045, src_046, src_048, and src_039 all imply that semantics-preserving structure optimization is the principled path to preserve exactness while reducing cost. In contrast, src_013, src_015, and src_033 explicitly push scalable approximations (truncation, approximate inference, scalable variants) and document efficiency gains with acknowledged effects on calibration or exactness. This is an extendable contradiction rather than a blocking one: AutoTW-ASP can absorb both views by treating approximation as a comparator lineage and maintaining exact routing targets where structure and reuse favor compilation.

Another contradiction concerns how much theory transfers to practice. ETH-based lower bounds in src_046 and src_047 show limited asymptotic room for generic treewidth-overhead elimination in normal ASP, while src_039 and the seed manuscript src_049 report practical benefits from structure-aware transformations and routing. These claims are not inconsistent if treated at different levels: worst-case complexity can remain hard while median-case workload distributions permit sizable gains. The downstream requirement is therefore methodological discipline: benchmark design must stratify by structural regimes, not just aggregate runtime, otherwise empirical improvements risk overclaiming beyond the measured instance distribution.

Equation-level comparison clarifies why rewrite design and routing should be coupled. The treewidth definition used in src_045 and src_039,
`treewidth(G) = min_{(T,chi)} max_t |chi(t)| - 1`, gives a direct optimization target for rewrite proposals. Source src_046 contributes an ETH-shaped lower-bound template of the form `2^{o(k*log k)} * poly(n)` being unattainable for generic normal ASP solving under assumptions, and src_047 links this hardness to cycles and reachability growth in SCC-sensitive constructions. Source src_048 expresses a counting-preserving bridge, `#ASP(Pi) = #SAT(Clark(Pi_FVS))` under correctness-preserving cycle breaking, demonstrating that specific preprocessing transforms can preserve counting objectives while moving instances toward tractable compiled representations. In neurosymbolic objectives, src_012 supplies a probabilistic stable-model factorization over neural atoms, and src_011 supplies neural-predicate probability parameterization in ProbLog-style semantics. AutoTW-ASP must therefore operate across two equation families: symbolic-structural bounds and probabilistic objective decompositions.

Assumption alignment is similarly important. The stable assumptions repeatedly used by treewidth-aware ASP papers are: normal program fragments, structure-aware reductions, and semantics-preserving transformations (src_045, src_046, src_048, src_039). Neurosymbolic training papers assume calibrated neural outputs and differentiability through logic-mediated objectives (src_011, src_012, src_016, src_033). The methodological gap appears where these assumption families meet: few sources fully specify an algorithm that (i) rewrites symbolic encodings under stable-model preservation guarantees, (ii) predicts post-rewrite structural gain, (iii) models compilation amortization via reuse, and (iv) routes each instance between enumeration and compilation during training. The seed manuscript src_049 points directly to this gap and frames reuse ratio plus structure proxies as routing signals, but it still leaves rewrite automation and crossover calibration as open development targets.

From a taxonomy perspective, the literature partitions into six operational blocks. Block one is graph-structural tractability theory (src_001, src_046, src_047). Block two is knowledge compilation and trace-to-circuit theory (src_002, src_003, src_006, src_008). Block three is weighted/algebraic counting as exact-inference substrate (src_004, src_007, src_009, src_022, src_039). Block four is neurosymbolic probabilistic logic frameworks (src_011, src_012, src_016, src_019, src_033, src_040). Block five is structure-aware ASP counting/reduction methods (src_045, src_046, src_048, src_039). Block six is broad surveys and robustness-centric synthesis (src_028, src_029, src_035, src_041, src_044). The project objective lives at the intersection of blocks three through five with evaluation obligations inherited from block six.

Consensus on baselines and comparator lineage is strong enough to define concrete evaluation structure. The immediate comparators are: naive encoding plus fixed backend, manually engineered low-treewidth or carry-style encodings, and scalable approximation-centric NP-ASP variants (src_033, src_015, src_013). The source record and user input both require ablations for rewrite quality, router feature contribution, and crossover behavior. To remain source-grounded, the router feature family should minimally include structural-width proxies, cycle/SCC diagnostics, and reuse indicators tied to compilation amortization (src_002, src_045, src_049). The expected downstream artifact is not only aggregate accuracy/runtime tables, but stratified analyses by structural regime that test whether gains persist when SCC size, treewidth, and reuse vary.

Methodological gaps remain substantial and should be treated as first-class research tasks. First, there is no canonical semantics-preserving rewrite library spanning default negation, integrity constraints, and arithmetic-relational patterns across all benchmark families. Second, existing papers provide partial but not unified guidance on crossover prediction; no source gives a mature learned router trained jointly on structural features, compilation traces, and reuse dynamics for neurosymbolic training loops. Third, reproducibility gaps persist at benchmark materialization level: split/version/checksum pinning for MNIST sum-k and 4x4 Sudoku is deferred, which blocks strong claims about robustness and generalization under repeated runs. These are blocking for final empirical claims, though not for hypothesis articulation.

The bridge opportunities that are defensible from sources are specific, not grand-unified. One bridge connects compilation-map language tradeoff principles (src_002) to backend routing features in neurosymbolic ASP (src_049): if target-language tractability and succinctness are operationalized as measurable instance features, routing can approximate compilation-map decisions online. A second bridge connects cycle-breaking equivalence in #ASP reductions (src_048) with rewrite-rule design: semantics-preserving transforms can be ranked by expected treewidth/SCC effect before committing to expensive compilation. A third bridge connects DPLL trace artifacts (src_003) with reuse-aware caching in training loops: trace or circuit reuse signals can serve as amortization predictors. Each bridge is plausible but unsettled and requires downstream validation artifacts.

The novelty boundary is therefore clear. Established facts from the corpus: structure controls exact inference cost; weighted/algebraic counting and compilation can preserve exact objectives under valid reductions; neurosymbolic systems benefit from symbolic constraints but struggle with scaling exact reasoning. Contested or regime-sensitive facts: the practical magnitude of treewidth-aware gains under diverse workloads; the extent to which approximation-centric scalability can preserve calibration and correctness guarantees needed by exact pipelines; and whether theoretical hardness barriers meaningfully limit practical rewrite+routing combinations. Defensible open contribution space: automatic stable-model-preserving rewrite synthesis plus dynamic backend routing calibrated by structure and reuse, evaluated against manual and approximation comparators with explicit ablations.

For downstream phases, notation should stay explicit and stable. Let `Pi` denote grounded normal ASP program, `O` observation/evidence, `R` rewrite operator from a semantics-preserving library, `Pi' = R(Pi)` rewritten program, `k(Pi')` structural-width proxy, `rho(Pi')` reuse ratio/circuit cache hit proxy, `C_enum` and `C_comp` predicted costs for enumeration and compilation backends, and `g(Pi', O)` router decision. Stable-model preservation should be formalized as `SM(Pi, O) ≡ SM(Pi', O)` for tasks where labels/supervision derive from stable models. Counting-preserving reductions can be represented as `Count_ASP(Pi') = Count_SAT(F(Pi'))` when reduction assumptions hold (src_048, src_039). The optimization target is expected training-time reduction under exactness and accuracy constraints, not unconstrained speed.

Uncertainty and robustness should be handled as measured outputs, not narrative claims. Survey and review sources (src_041, src_044, src_029, src_035) repeatedly emphasize robustness and uncertainty calibration gaps in neuro-symbolic systems. In this project, the uncertainty question maps to whether routing decisions remain reliable under distribution shift in structural features (for example, larger `k`, different SCC profiles, lower reuse). Robustness maps to whether semantics-preserving rewrites and routing avoid accuracy regressions on held-out regimes and preserve exactness checks. These concerns are extendable but become blocking if omitted from experiment acceptance criteria.

In conclusion, the distilled literature supports a strong and testable research program: an algorithmic optimization layer for neurosymbolic ASP that combines semantics-preserving rewrite synthesis and structure-aware backend routing. The corpus provides enough theoretical grounding for why structure-aware optimization should help (src_001, src_045, src_046, src_047, src_048, src_039), enough systems grounding for why neurosymbolic exactness is valuable but expensive (src_011, src_012, src_019, src_033), and enough methodological warning signals to prevent overclaiming (src_013, src_015, src_033, src_041, src_044). What remains open, and therefore publication-worthy, is to close the algorithm-to-evidence gap with explicit rewrite rules, calibrated router design, ablations over structural regimes, and reproducible benchmark materialization that validates gains beyond handcrafted encodings.
